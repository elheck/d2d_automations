Indicator Reference

Pricing Signals are shown above the chart automatically. Toggle additional lines in the chart legend to overlay them.

1d/7d Momentum
avg1 − avg7. Positive = short-term spike, negative = recent drop. Best signal for whether a card is actively moving right now.
7d/30d Momentum
avg7 − avg30. Positive = trending up vs last month, negative = cooling off. Use to confirm if short-term momentum has legs.
Floor Ratio
low ÷ trend. Above 0.9 = healthy, listings close to trend. Below 0.8 = heavy undercutting. If floor is low, price near it or wait.
Price Position (BB %B)
Where current price sits in its historical range. 0% = at lower band (cheap vs history), 100% = at upper band (expensive). Above 80% = consider pricing high. Below 20% = suppressed, may recover.
ROC 7d / ROC 30d
Rate of Change — the % price move over 7 or 30 days. Directly answers "how much has this moved?" Positive = gained, negative = lost.
RSI (chart toggle)
Relative Strength Index (0–100). Above 70 = overbought (price may drop soon). Below 30 = oversold (may recover). Useful for timing. Needs 14+ data points.
MACD / Signal (chart toggle)
Momentum crossover. When MACD crosses above Signal = bullish. Below = bearish. Needs 26+ data points to produce values.
Bollinger Bands (chart toggle)
Upper/Lower bands show ±2 standard deviations from the 20-day average. Price touching lower band = historically cheap. Touching upper = expensive. Needs 20+ data points.
EMA 7 / EMA 30 (chart toggle)
Exponential Moving Averages. EMA 7 reacts fast to recent changes. EMA 30 shows the longer trend. When EMA 7 crosses above EMA 30 = bullish signal.
